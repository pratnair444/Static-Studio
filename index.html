// 01 — The Position

WHY WE
EXIST

What We Aim To Do

Our mission, plainly stated

We study brands the way a critic studies film — with genuine obsession and zero sentimentality. We look for what's invisible, what's working underneath the surface, and what's quietly leaking value.

Then we say it out loud. Clearly. Without softening it.

  • Identify what your brand actually communicates vs. what you think it does
  • Find the gap between the audience you have and the audience you deserve
  • Build marketing that leaves a mark, not just an impression
  • Tell you the thing your last agency was afraid to say

What Brands Should Do

The uncomfortable truth

Your brand narrative is not yours to ignore. The market will write it for you the moment you stop. And the version they write is rarely flattering.

Brands that last don't just respond to culture — they move before it does. That takes nerve, not just a content calendar.

  • Stop optimising for reach and start optimising for recall
  • Own a feeling, not just a category
  • Let your actual point of view show — audiences smell the absence of one
  • Treat every touchpoint as a vote for what your brand believes

On The Links

Sports Bar · Indoor Golf · Brand Positioning

Project 02 · Thunder Bay

The Conservatory

Community Asset · Cultural Strategy · Youth Outreach

Positioned a city conservatory not just as a public asset, but as a pipeline for inspiration — particularly for Indigenous youth. Connected environmental engagement with long-term STEM exposure, framing the space as one that sparks curiosity in biology, sustainability, and environmental sciences.

Project 03 · Thunder Bay

Thunder Bay Public Library

Civic Institution · Modernization · Digital Strategy

The challenge was modernization without identity loss. We proposed structural updates to how the library presents itself publicly — refreshing marketing channels, improving service visibility, and rethinking how it connects with younger demographics. The goal was to evolve an aging system into a more digitally aware, community-responsive institution while preserving its role as a civic anchor.
